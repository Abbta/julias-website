I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the Department of Economics at Brown University. I will be on the job market in 2020-2021 and attend the ASSA Meetings and EEA Meetings

My research focuses on public and labor economics, with a focus on intergenerational transmission of economic prospects.

In my job market paper I study how economic shocks to parents affect the educational outcomes and career paths of children.
Job Market Paper
Parent layoffs and children’s careers
Abstract:
We analyse the effect of a parent's layoff on the educational outcome of their children. We use administrative data on layoffs caused by shocks to labor demand and variation in child age at the time of parent layoff to estimate the age-specific impact on high school graduation rates. We find that parent layoff may have a large impact on high school completion rates, but the effect is concentrated to children who are about to apply to high school. A parent layoff in the month before the school choice deadline decreases the likelihood for the child to finish high school on time by 9 percentage points relative to a layoff in the same school semester, but after the deadline. The effect is higher for low-information families, consistent with the hypothesis that making the right choice may be costly, even if the final good is free.
we match families with parent layoffs to control families who are connected to a firm with layoffs. We observe a decrease in likelihood of graduating high school on time (at age 19) for children whose parents get laid off when the child is 14 to 16 years old.
To estimate selection into layoff, we add families with older children at the time of layoff. A parental layoff at age 21 can not affect high school graduation at age 19.
